## Decoding the Network Routing — What Those Acronyms Actually Mean

If you've read this far, you've seen a pile of network terminology. Here's the plain-language version:

- **CN2 GIA** — China Telecom's premium Global Internet Access line. Lower latency and more stable than standard China Telecom routing. This is what makes "China-optimised" plans actually feel different from a generic US VPS.
- **AS9929** — China Unicom's premium backbone route (the "9929" is the ASN). Same idea: optimized path for Unicom traffic.
- **CMIN2** — China Mobile International Network 2. The Mobile equivalent of the above.
- **IIJ** — Internet Initiative Japan, one of Japan's most reputable upstream providers. Used on the Osaka plans. Solid for Asia-Pacific connectivity.
- **BGP** — Border Gateway Protocol. On the Hong Kong plans, this means multi-line BGP routing with CN2 inbound — good for HK-origin traffic.
- **International / Global** — Standard international transit (NTT, Orange, Cogent, etc.). No China optimization. The LA Global and VDS lines use this.

**Why this matters for coupon shoppers:** the China-optimised plans (LA AMD Optimised, LA AMD VPS, LA Intel, LA Ryzen9, Hong Kong, Tokyo) are the ones where the 8NU44CM6LZ coupon is reported to apply. The International and Falkenstein lines typically don't qualify. If your workload doesn't touch China at all, you probably don't need the optimized routing — and you might find the Global plans give you more raw bandwidth per dollar.
